## GRB 110721A

1. *GRB 110721A: An Extreme Peak Energy and Signatures of the Photosphere* — The Fermi LAT + GBM collaboration plus Pe’er. ([arXiv][1])

   * They model the time-resolved spectrum with **Band + blackbody**, find extremely high initial (E_{\rm peak}) (~15 MeV), and study temperature evolution.
   * Very relevant for your work on BB dominance.

2. *Variable Jet Properties in GRB 110721A: Time Resolved Spectroscopy* by S. Iyyani et al. (2013) ([OUP Academic][2])

   * They explore deviations from a simple Band spectrum, discuss the presence of multiple spectral peaks and possible jet-structure effects.

3. *Photospheric Emission in Gamma-ray Bursts* (thesis by C. Lundman, includes GRB 110721A) ([DIVA Portal][3])

   * Useful for theoretical modeling of photospheric broadening, angular effects, and interpretation of thermal + nonthermal mixing.

4. *GRB 110721A: Photosphere “Death Line” and Physical Constraints* (by Zhang & Lu) ([Semantic Scholar][4])

   * Discusses constraints on photospheric emission and where the “death line” (limit) for thermal emission lies relative to observed parameters.

## GRB 080916C

1. *Fermi Observations of High-Energy Gamma-Ray Emission from GRB 080916C* (Abdo et al., Science 2009) ([Science][5])

   * A foundational observational reference: how the prompt emission extended to very high energies, the spectral features across GBM + LAT.

2. *GRB 080916C: On the Radiation Origin of the Prompt Emission from keV/MeV to GeV* by Wang, Li, Dai, Mészáros ([arXiv][6])

   * They argue for a **synchrotron origin** spanning MeV → tens of GeV, placing constraints on electron cooling and spectral slopes.

3. *Evidence of an Initially Magnetically Dominated Outflow in GRB 080916C* by Bing Zhang & Asaf Pe’er ([arXiv][7])

   * Because GRB 080916C lacked a strong thermal component, they use that to argue for a Poynting-flux dominated outflow rather than a pure baryonic fireball.

4. *Time Resolved Spectral Analysis of Prompt Emission of Long GRBs with GeV Emission* (Rao et al. 2013) ([arXiv][8])

   * They include GRB 080916C and fit composite models (e.g. two BB + power-law) in addition to Band, study spectral evolution, temperature correlations etc.

5. *Prompt GeV Emission from Residual Collisions in GRB Outflows* by Zhuo Li (for GRB 080916C) ([arXiv][9])

   * They propose that high-energy photons come from residual collisions (IC scattering), and derive a bulk Lorentz factor (~300) based on time delays and spectral breaks.
